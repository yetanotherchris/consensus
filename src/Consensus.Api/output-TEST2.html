Overview and caution

  • The Five-Factor Model (OCEAN) shows robust, replicable links with DSM personality disorders (PDs), especially at the extremes of each trait or facet. Associations are probabilistic, not deterministic, and vary across subtypes; the strongest, most general correlate of PD is high Neuroticism/Negative Emotionality 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002.

Neuroticism (Negative Emotionality)

  • Prominent maladaptive facets: anxiousness, angry hostility, depressiveness, vulnerability, impulsiveness.
  • Personality disorders most consistently linked:
    • Borderline PD: very strong elevations across neuroticism facets; typically accompanied by lower conscientiousness (impulsivity) and lower agreeableness (conflict) 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Avoidant PD: high anxiety and self-consciousness; often combined with low extraversion (detachment) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002.
    • Dependent PD: high anxiousness and vulnerability; often higher agreeableness facets (compliance) 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Paranoid PD: high angry hostility with low trust (low agreeableness) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Schizotypal PD: elevations in anxiousness with unconventionality/open experience facets (see Openness below) 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002.
    • Narcissistic PD: “vulnerable” presentations show higher neuroticism than “grandiose” presentations 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002.

Extraversion (vs. Detachment)

  • Prominent maladaptive poles: very low extraversion (detachment: low warmth, gregariousness, positive affect) or very high assertiveness/excitement-seeking.
  • Personality disorders most consistently linked:
    • Schizoid and Schizotypal PDs: very low warmth, gregariousness, and positive emotions (detachment) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Avoidant PD: low assertiveness and gregariousness (social inhibition) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002.
    • Histrionic and Narcissistic PDs: higher assertiveness, gregariousness, and excitement-seeking (social boldness) 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Antisocial PD: higher excitement-seeking/activity combined with low agreeableness and low conscientiousness 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002.

Openness to Experience (vs. Closedness/Psychoticism)

  • Prominent maladaptive poles: extreme openness to unusual perceptions/ideas (psychoticism-like) vs. marked rigidity/closedness to new experiences.
  • Personality disorders most consistently linked:
    • Schizotypal PD: higher openness facets (fantasy, ideas, unconventional beliefs/perceptual dysregulation), aligning with DSM-5 Psychoticism 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401; trait-model evidence: 10.1017/S0033291712002408; 10.1016/j.jrp.2013.05.004.
    • Obsessive–Compulsive PD: lower openness to actions, values, and feelings (cognitive-behavioral rigidity), often with high conscientiousness 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002.
    • Paranoid PD: some evidence for lower openness to values/ideas (closed-mindedness), though findings are mixed across studies 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002.

Agreeableness (vs. Antagonism)

  • Prominent maladaptive poles: very low agreeableness (antagonism: callousness, deceitfulness, grandiosity) vs. excessively high compliance/deference.
  • Personality disorders most consistently linked:
    • Antisocial PD: very low agreeableness across facets (low trust, straightforwardness, altruism; hostile/callous) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Narcissistic PD: low modesty and tender-mindedness; antagonistic grandiosity alongside assertive extraversion 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Paranoid PD: low trust and straightforwardness (suspiciousness) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002.
    • Borderline PD: moderate antagonism (particularly low compliance) co-occurring with high neuroticism 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Dependent PD: higher agreeableness (compliance/modesty) together with high neuroticism 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002.

Conscientiousness (vs. Disinhibition/Compulsivity)

  • Prominent maladaptive poles: very low conscientiousness (impulsivity, irresponsibility) or very high conscientiousness (overcontrol, perfectionism).
  • Personality disorders most consistently linked:
    • Antisocial PD: low dutifulness, deliberation, and self-discipline (disinhibition) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.
    • Borderline PD: lower self-discipline and deliberation (impulsivity), especially alongside high neuroticism 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002.
    • Histrionic PD: lower deliberation/self-discipline (impulsivity/attention-seeking) 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002.
    • Obsessive–Compulsive PD: very high orderliness, dutifulness, and deliberation (compulsivity) together with low openness (rigidity) 10.1016/j.cpr.2007.04.002; 10.1016/j.cpr.2004.01.002.
    • Schizoid/Schizotypal PDs: often lower competence and achievement striving (amotivation) in facet-level prototypes 10.1037/0021-843X.110.3.401.

Crosswalk to DSM-5 Alternative Model for Personality Disorders (AMPD)

  • The AMPD’s trait domains align closely with OCEAN: Negative Affectivity ≈ high Neuroticism; Detachment ≈ low Extraversion; Antagonism ≈ low Agreeableness; Disinhibition vs. Compulsivity ≈ low vs. high Conscientiousness; Psychoticism ≈ extreme Openness to unusual perceptions and beliefs 10.1017/S0033291712002408; 10.1016/j.jrp.2013.05.004.
